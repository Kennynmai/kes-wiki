# Bathtub Filter KES Route Elimination Memo — V1

## Why this page exists
This page is stricter than a route-screening memo.
Its job is to say which routes KES should **de-prioritize, avoid, or only keep under narrow conditions**.

## Elimination logic
### Eliminate as primary route
#### 1. Aggressive eczema / baby-outcome DTC route
Why eliminate:
- highest claim/compliance risk
- strongest trust-collapse downside if benefit is ambiguous
- easiest route to drift into language KES should not want to defend

KES posture:
- do **not** use as primary launch logic
- at most, keep as a later messaging-adjacent learning zone, not a core product route

#### 2. Commodity broad-claim bath-ball clone
Why eliminate:
- easy to copy, hard to defend
- generic marketplace competition is likely brutal
- broad contaminant stack invites skepticism and weakens trust
- fit and chlorine-efficacy disputes already surface in public signals

KES posture:
- do **not** enter as a me-too commodity bath-ball unless proof and differentiation become unusually strong

### Keep only under strict conditions
#### 3. Premium baby/wellness design route
Why not eliminate fully:
- real emotional and commercial upside exists
- gifting / registry / social-friendly positioning is attractive

Why constrain:
- premium price increases review scrutiny
- soft authority language can become proof theater
- performance must survive more skeptical audiences

KES posture:
- only continue if normal-flow proof, fit discipline, and claim restraint are all stronger than what current market leaders visibly show

#### 4. Soft-hanging ritual route
Why not eliminate fully:
- differentiated look and softer lifestyle feel
- may fit a KES-adjacent premium bathroom story better than hard bath-ball aesthetics

Why constrain:
- drying, storage, refill cadence, and overflow/fill-speed frustration can become structural user burden

KES posture:
- continue only if maintenance friction can be made acceptably low and clearly explainable

### Keep as strongest surviving candidates
#### 5. Hybrid premium-but-disciplined route
Why keep:
- best balance between credibility and commercial appeal
- avoids both generic low-trust commodity and aggressive overclaim DTC extremes

KES posture:
- strongest current survivor

#### 6. Narrow chlorine-focused / technically disciplined route
Why keep:
- most defensible claim territory surfaced so far
- easier to align with realistic proof and compatibility discipline

KES posture:
- strongest technical-discipline template, whether or not KES copies the visual language directly

## Current route stack for KES
### Eliminate / avoid as lead route
- aggressive eczema / baby-outcome DTC
- commodity broad-claim bath-ball clone

### Conditional only
- premium baby/wellness design route
- soft-hanging ritual route

### Surviving best candidates
- hybrid premium-but-disciplined route
- narrow chlorine-focused / technically disciplined route

## Best current strategic read
KES should probably spend less time asking:
> what route sounds most exciting?

and more time asking:
> which route still looks good after claim discipline, normal-flow proof, fit realism, and complaint-pattern risk are applied?
