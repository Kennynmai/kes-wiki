# Bathtub Filter Claim Risk Audit — V2

| Claim class | Example surfaced wording | Source anchor | Evidence posture | Risk | KES posture |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Free chlorine reduction | “helps to remove free chlorine” | Sprite / NSF-177-adjacent shower context | relatively strongest | low-medium | usable with precise wording |
| Faster-flow chlorine performance | “the only product to reduce 100% chlorine at the faster flow” | WaterFilterGuru on Santevia | stronger than generic chlorine claims if independently replicated | medium | valuable differentiator if reproducible |
| Broad contaminant stack | heavy metals, VOCs, PFAS, pesticides, hundreds of contaminants | Crystal Quest / marketplace generic content | often broad and route-unspecific | high | avoid unless tightly validated |
| Sensitive-skin comfort | gentler bath, spa-like, more comfortable | Canopy / lifestyle/editorial / product pages | commercial but indirect | medium | usable if not medicalized |
| Baby-safe / parent reassurance | “the bath filter every parent needs” / “worth the $89 price tag” for baby bath gear | Instagram / Motherly / Canopy | emotionally powerful, evidence-light | high | high caution |
| Improve skin hydration | “clinically tested to improve skin hydration” | Kinder surfaced copy | demands stronger substantiation than surfaced in this pass | very high | treat as red-flag language |
| Eczema improvement | “alleviate… eczema” / eczema-friendly hooks | Kinder / Tubo / social hooks | strongest commercial temptation, weakest clearly verified finished-product proof | very high | avoid direct efficacy claims |
| Clinical/proven superiority | “CLINICALLY TESTED” / “proven” | Tubo / Kinder | context often insufficiently visible | very high | audit aggressively |
| Hard-water softening | softens hard water / balances hard water | marketplace and some broad content | weak support for compact bathtub-filter systems | high | likely avoid |
| Certified vs tested ambiguity | “tested to NSF 177” vs certified | WaterFilterGuru on Canopy shower context | critical wording distinction | high | always separate tested-from-certified |

## Working conclusion
The most commercially magnetic wording in this category is often the least defensible. KES should treat baby/eczema/clinical language as the highest-scrutiny zone.
