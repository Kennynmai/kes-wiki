# Egypt Building Materials Cooperation Framework Agreement - 2025-10-20

## Why this source matters

This framework agreement defines the early partnership economics for the Egypt building-materials project. It is the reference document for interpreting whether early expenses are research-stage costs, stage-2 operating/capital contributions, wage-offset contributions, or later equity/profit-entitlement inputs.

## Key points

- Parties: Mai Kefeng and Zhang Taotao.
- Project structure: Mai sets up a Hong Kong holding company, which controls the Egypt operating company. The Egypt company is the operating/liability entity.
- Visa-support equity: up to 10% of holding-company equity may be given to business participants for Egypt work-visa needs; this equity is counted in the holding company but does not participate in dividends.
- Zhang role: Egypt execution lead, responsible for daily operation.
- Stage 1 research-stage expenses: Mai 80%, Zhang 20%; entrusted nature; no equity and no capital contribution.
- Stage 2 virtual-equity contribution ratio: Mai 80%, Zhang 20%.
- Stage 2 contribution plan: cash contribution in four installments, about six months per installment. Zhang must fully pay the first two installments in cash at the 20% ratio; the last two installments may combine wage-offset and cash as separately agreed.
- Zhang salary and wage-offset: RMB 25,000/month total, with RMB 10,000 paid in cash and RMB 15,000 counted as wage-offset. The wage-offset part counts toward `工资折抵累计额`, not actual cash contribution.
- Missed contribution consequence: virtual equity is recalculated by cumulative actual contribution ratio, without affecting Mai's control; the recalculation is only for stage-2 dividend reference.
- Expense authority: Zhang may approve single expenses of RMB 2,000 or below; higher expenses require Mai approval.
- Finance authority: Zhang does not participate in company financial operations and has information rights only.
- Stage-2 distribution: after positive cash flow, reserve three months of operating expenses; remaining net cash flow is calculated annually on January 1. 10% goes to frontline employee bonus pool, 20% to management bonus pool. No dividend in year one; from year two, distribution follows contribution ratio.
- Stage-2 Zhang profit right: reference profit entitlement only, not actual shareholder rights, voting rights, or residual asset rights.
- Stage 3 formal agreement: later `Definitive Agreement` replaces this framework and prior supplements. Zhang's actual holding-company equity will be negotiated based on actual cash contribution, wage-offset cumulative amount, operating performance, and business contribution.
- Valuation and exit: internal financing valuation uses 5x the Hong Kong holding company's average net cash flow over the prior two years; external financing valuation cannot be lower. If Zhang exits, Mai has priority purchase right; price range is between audited net asset value and the 5x average-net-cash-flow valuation.

## Caveats / tensions

- The PDF has no embedded text layer. The raw markdown is OCR extracted and spot-checked, but exact legal use should still compare against the signed scan.
- The current expense sheet uses department labels rather than explicit Stage 1 / Stage 2 classifications. The ledger should not automatically treat all `投资部` rows as Zhang's final capital contribution without applying the agreement's stage and approval rules.
- Stage 1 says expenses are shared 80/20 but not capital contribution; Stage 2 says capital is 80/20 and split into installments. These are different buckets and should not be collapsed during reconciliation.

## Suggested wiki updates

- Keep this agreement linked to the project ledger so future cost allocation can be checked against the 80/20 research-stage rule and stage-2 contribution logic.
- Add a reconciliation protocol later: classify each expense by stage, approval status, original currency, receipt evidence, and whether it is expense reimbursement, capital contribution, or wage-offset contribution.
