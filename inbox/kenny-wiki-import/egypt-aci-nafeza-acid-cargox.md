# Egypt ACI / NAFEZA / ACID / CargoX

## Scope
This page summarizes the Egypt Advance Cargo Information flow as it matters for exporting goods to Egypt, especially for a China-to-Egypt supply chain supporting an Egypt manufacturing or distribution project.

It is not legal advice. Before shipment, confirm the current process with the Egyptian importer, customs broker, freight forwarder, NAFEZA, and CargoX.

## Key terms

### NAFEZA
NAFEZA is Egypt's national single-window platform for foreign trade. Egyptian importers use it to register import shipments, submit advance data, request ACID numbers, and support customs release.

### ACI
Advance Cargo Information is Egypt's pre-shipment customs procedure. Cargo data and documents must be available before shipment so customs can perform risk screening before goods arrive.

For sea freight, ACI has been mandatory since 2021-10-01. For air freight, NAFEZA states that the trial phase continues through 2025-12-31 and mandatory operation starts on 2026-01-01.

### ACID
ACID is the Advance Cargo Information Declaration number generated by NAFEZA after the Egyptian importer registers the shipment. CargoX describes it as a unique 19-digit number. It links the shipment registered in NAFEZA with the documents filed by the foreign exporter through CargoX.

### CargoX
CargoX is the certified blockchain document transfer provider for Egypt ACI. Foreign exporters register and verify their company on CargoX, then use it to submit the ACI envelope and shipment documents to NAFEZA.

## Practical flow
1. Egyptian importer registers on NAFEZA and uses an e-signature/e-token.
2. Foreign exporter registers and completes company verification on CargoX.
3. Importer enters exporter and shipment data in NAFEZA and requests the ACID.
4. NAFEZA issues the ACID and notifies the importer/exporter.
5. Exporter uploads required documents on CargoX, enters the ACID, seals/transfers the ACI envelope, and sends it to NAFEZA.
6. Customs uses the pre-arrival data for risk screening and later release.

## Documents usually required
NAFEZA lists the usual ACI documents as:
- commercial invoice, including PDF and XLS/XLSX in NAFEZA template or converted Excel form;
- packing list;
- bill of lading copy for sea shipments, or air waybill copy for air shipments;
- certificate of origin;
- other shipment-specific documents when applicable, such as analysis, fumigation, inspection, insurance, halal, health, MSDS, phytosanitary, veterinary, or similar certificates.

CargoX notes that each document should be uploaded separately with the correct document type, and that one document should not exceed 5 MB.

## Cost notes
CargoX states that ACI fees are paid by the exporter. As of the checked CargoX pages:
- sea ACI filing fee: 160 CargoX units per ACID;
- air ACI filing fee: 80 units per ACID from 2026-01-01 to 2026-06-30;
- document transfer fee: 3 units per document, capped at 15 units per ACID envelope;
- CargoX company verification also requires units.

Treat these as operating assumptions only; pricing is a live service-policy item.

## Risks and failure modes
- If the foreign exporter is not verified on CargoX, the importer may be unable to complete a usable ACI process.
- ACID must be obtained before the exporter files the ACI envelope.
- Wrong exporter identity, tax/registration data, invoice data, HS code, bill of lading data, or document type can delay clearance.
- For air freight, the ACID must be placed on the air waybill in the relevant customs-information field.
- ACI does not remove the need to validate ordinary customs issues: HS classification, duty, VAT, import permits, standards, certificates, and physical inspection risk.

## Implications for the Egypt factory project
- For Chinese equipment, fabric, accessories, rails, packaging, and spare parts shipped into Egypt, ACI should be treated as a front-end operating dependency, not a back-office afterthought.
- The Egyptian company/importer, Chinese supplier/exporter, freight forwarder, and customs broker need a shared pre-shipment data checklist before goods leave China.
- The purchasing process should capture all data needed for NAFEZA and CargoX: supplier legal identity, CargoX ID, invoice number/date/value/currency, HS codes, origin, weights, package count, port/airport data, and transport document details.
- The first shipment should be run as a controlled pilot, because account verification, ACID issuance, document formatting, and broker coordination are likely to reveal process friction.
