# Raw Capture — Bathtub Filter Second- and Third-Tier Scouting (2026-04-12)

## Purpose
Expand the topic beyond first-tier evidence/review/compliance work into:
- installation / compatibility engineering
- pricing / replacement / refill logic
- channel / retailer landscape
- SNS / creator / visual-merchandising signals

## Installation / compatibility signals
- WaterFilterGuru snippets suggest several bathtub filters require slower faucet flow to maintain meaningful chlorine reduction.
- Crystal Quest visible pages emphasize broad fit and tool-free install, but review-like snippets also suggest flimsy plastics / stability concerns can matter.
- Community and explainer content surfaced workaround logic:
  - hose-based bath fill via showerhead routes
  - compatibility dependence on faucet shape/type
- implication:
  - “universal fit” language is probably one of the most fragile commercial promises in the category
  - true category performance may depend on a three-way interaction among faucet geometry, attachment method, and realistic fill speed

## Pricing / replacement / refill signals
- multiple bathtub-filter listings cluster around **2,500 gallons** as a common cartridge-life number
- surfaced replacement expectations vary materially:
  - about **4–6 months** in some generic/replacement listings
  - about **6 months** in some bath replacement listings
  - **12–18 months** or much higher gallon numbers in some Crystal Quest-linked/reseller materials
- implication:
  - replacement economics and cadence may be highly inconsistent or weakly standardized
  - cartridge-life claims likely need a later sanity check against realistic household bath usage

## SNS / creator / visual signals
### YouTube / creator review layer
- YouTube has clear review and “worth it?” content, especially around Canopy and best-bath-filter comparisons
- creator content frequently frames the category around:
  - baby bathing
  - chlorine fear / reduction
  - premium design vs actual performance

### Instagram / short-form social signals
- surfaced snippets indicate direct parent / eczema hooks such as “the bath filter every parent needs” and “eczema-friendly bath filter”
- implication:
  - short-form social seems to compress the category into a very simple emotional promise: gentler baths for babies / eczema-prone kids

### Visual-merchandising clue
Even without full image analysis yet, surfaced titles/descriptions suggest at least three visual strategies:
1. **soft baby-safe / gentle-parenting aesthetic**
2. **premium bathroom / elevated ritual aesthetic**
3. **technical / filtration-performance aesthetic**

## Strategic implication
The second and third tiers matter because they answer the “last mile” questions the first-tier work cannot answer:
- does the product fit and work in real bathrooms?
- is refill cadence commercially acceptable?
- which channel identity is strongest?
- what emotional/visual story actually closes the sale?
