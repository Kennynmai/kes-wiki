# Bathtub Filter Channel Positioning Table

## Table
| Channel type | Representative surfaces | Typical message | Likely winning angle | Likely weakness |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Marketplace | Amazon, Walmart | removes chlorine / contaminants / softer skin | breadth, price competition, bundles, quick conversion | overclaiming, generic lookalikes, weak trust |
| Home improvement / utility retail | Home Depot | fit, replacement, practical use, hardware adjacency | utilitarian trust, replacement ecosystem | less emotionally resonant than baby/wellness DTC |
| Premium DTC wellness | Canopy, Santevia | gentler bath, premium ritual, family comfort | design, content control, registry/gifting, lifestyle | premium pricing scrutiny |
| Baby / sensitive-skin DTC | Kinder, Tubo | eczema-friendly, safer bath time, parent reassurance | emotional urgency and segment specificity | highest claim/compliance risk |
| Reseller / educator / reviewer | WaterFilterGuru, wellness resellers, parenting media | explanation + recommendation | bridges skepticism and conversion | mixed incentives and variable rigor |

## Strategic interpretation
The category is not just sold through different stores; it is **reframed into different product identities** depending on channel.

That means KES cannot evaluate bathtub filters as a single positioning problem.
