# Egypt Furniture and Export Market Snapshot - 2026-05-10

## Bottom line
Egyptian furniture exports are not mainly a US/EU story. They are mainly a nearby Arab-market story, with Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Sudan, Libya, the UAE, and the US reported as the top six destination markets for Egyptian furniture in 2024.

Damietta is the industrial center of gravity. Official Egyptian sources describe Damietta Furniture City as 54 hangars containing 1,348 workshops/factories plus technology and service centers. Ahram Online, citing the Egyptian Furniture Export Council's analysis of GOEIC data, reported 2024 furniture exports of about USD 343 million, up 8% from 2023, with Damietta accounting for about 70% of sector exports and Arab markets absorbing about 80%.

For the US, Egypt's overall exports are dominated by apparel/textiles and related light manufacturing, not furniture. For China and East/Southeast Asia, Egypt's exports are mostly commodities, agricultural products, cotton/textile inputs, fuels, minerals, and some apparel; furniture is small to negligible. In furniture, "Asia" primarily means West Asia / Arab markets, not China, Japan, Korea, or ASEAN.

## Furniture export destinations

### Main destination pattern
The strongest furniture demand appears to be in nearby Arab markets:
- Saudi Arabia: largest visible HS 94 furniture destination in 2024.
- Iraq: second major furniture destination.
- Sudan, Libya, UAE: meaningful regional demand.
- US: present but much smaller than the Arab cluster.

The Ahram / EFEC country list for 2024 is:
- Saudi Arabia
- Iraq
- Sudan
- Libya
- UAE
- US

An SIS report on EFEC's January-September 2025 update listed top importers as Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Libya, UAE, US, Greece, Syria, and Algeria. This implies the same regional core continued into 2025, with some European and Levant/North Africa additions.

### Approximate 2024 HS 94 destination values
Using UN Comtrade-derived Trading Economics pages for broad HS 94 furniture/lighting/prefab:
- Saudi Arabia: about USD 90.6 million.
- Iraq: about USD 46.8 million.
- Sudan: about USD 24.5 million.
- UAE: about USD 12.6 million based on UAE-reported imports from Egypt, or lower depending on reporter direction.
- US: about USD 9.9 million from Egypt-reported exports to the US.
- China: about USD 2.1 million in 2025, mostly lamps/lighting, with furniture itself tiny.

These values are useful for directional sizing, but should not be over-precise because reporter-country data and Egypt-reported data can differ.

## Furniture product mix

### Dominant categories
Egypt's furniture export mix is heavily wooden and "other furniture" rather than mass global commodity seating:
- HS 940360, other wooden furniture: USD 187.3 million in 2024, led by Saudi Arabia, Iraq, Libya, UAE, Sudan, and the US.
- HS 940320, metal furniture: USD 33.3 million in 2024, led by Saudi Arabia, Germany, US, Oman, and Italy.
- HS 940350, wooden bedroom furniture: USD 16.8 million in 2024, unusually concentrated in Sudan.
- HS 940330, wooden office furniture: USD 6.8 million in 2024, led by UAE, Saudi Arabia, Nigeria, Sudan, and US.
- HS 940340, wooden kitchen furniture: USD 1.6 million in 2024, led by Sudan, Oman, US, Saudi Arabia, and UAE.
- HS 940161/940169, wooden-frame seats: small export values relative to global furniture trade.

### Style and material interpretation
The likely commercial positioning is:
- Wood-centered home furniture: bedrooms, dining rooms, salons/living rooms, wardrobes, office furniture, custom room sets.
- Classical / ornate carved wooden furniture: smaller in volume but still commercially visible, including unfinished or semi-finished carved pieces sold to importers who complete upholstery, painting, and finishing abroad.
- Modern / client-reference designs: workshops produce modern designs from customer reference photos, with separate carpentry, upholstery, and painting workshops cooperating.
- Project/custom work: hotel, office, institutional, and bespoke interiors appear in Damietta manufacturer positioning.
- Materials: natural wood and wood frames are the main identity; MDF boards are noted as an important imported raw material, with Egypt attempting to expand local MDF production. Upholstery, paint, sponge/foam, adhesives, accessories, metal furniture, and lighting/prefab sit around the core furniture industry.

## Egypt exports to the US
Egypt's overall exports to the US are mainly garments and textile-adjacent products.

2025 Egypt-reported exports to the US were about USD 2.71 billion. Leading categories:
- non-knit apparel: USD 760.1 million;
- knit apparel: USD 574.2 million;
- ships/boats/floating structures: USD 175.4 million;
- prepared vegetables/fruits/nuts: USD 132.9 million;
- salt/sulphur/stone/cement: USD 107.3 million;
- carpets and textile floor coverings: USD 106.5 million;
- iron and steel: USD 82.4 million;
- vegetables/roots/tubers: USD 71.9 million;
- oil seeds/grains/seeds/fruits: USD 71.4 million;
- glass and glassware: USD 62.3 million.

US-reported imports from Egypt for 2025 were about USD 3.06 billion, with a similar but not identical ranking: knit apparel, non-knit apparel, iron/steel, stone/cement, prepared food, fruit/nuts, carpets, fertilizers, vegetables, and glass.

Furniture is not a major US-bound Egyptian export category: broad HS 94 to the US was about USD 9.9 million in 2024, mostly "other furniture" plus bedding/furnishing, lighting, and a small amount of seats.

### Industrial products exported to the US
Egypt's larger industrial/manufactured exports to the US are:
- apparel: knit apparel about USD 919.9 million and non-knit apparel about USD 440.6 million in 2025 by US import data;
- iron and steel: about USD 190.2 million in 2025 by US import data; 2024 detail was mainly hot-rolled/non-alloy steel bars and rods, plus flat-rolled steel and ferro-alloys;
- cement/salt/stone/mineral materials: about USD 176.3 million in 2025; 2024 detail was mostly Portland and similar hydraulic cement plus salt/sodium chloride;
- carpets and textile floor coverings: about USD 126.6 million in 2025;
- fertilizers: about USD 121.5 million in 2025;
- glass and glassware: about USD 74.6 million in 2025;
- plastics: about USD 75.5 million in 2024, including plastic sheets/film/foil/strip, polyethers/polyesters in primary forms, plastic sanitary ware, and packaging/other plastic articles.

Egypt-reported 2025 exports also show a large ships/boats/floating-structures line item. Because this does not appear symmetrically in the US import top list, treat it cautiously as a possible timing, reporting, re-export, repair/temporary movement, or classification issue rather than a stable Egyptian manufacturing strength without shipment-level confirmation.

### Are Egypt's US textile exports Egyptian-cotton products?
Not mostly. The US-bound textile/apparel story is primarily an apparel-manufacturing and trade-preference story, not simply an Egyptian-cotton raw-material story.

Evidence:
- US imports from Egypt of finished apparel were over USD 1.36 billion in 2025, while US imports from Egypt under the cotton chapter were only about USD 3.75 million.
- The dominant US apparel imports from Egypt are finished garments such as sweaters/pullovers, men's and women's suits/trousers/dresses, T-shirts, shirts, pajamas, and similar apparel categories. These can be cotton, synthetic, blends, or other fibers depending on HS8/HTS detail and buyer spec.
- Egypt's QIZ program is important for US-bound apparel. As of September 2025, AmCham Egypt reported 1,293 QIZ companies; about 80% produce apparel/accessories, and QIZ products represented about 50% of Egypt's total exports to the US in 2025. QIZ eligibility is based on value-added and Israeli input requirements, not a requirement that the fabric be Egyptian cotton.

Practical reading:
- Some Egyptian apparel or home-textile products may use Egyptian cotton, especially premium cotton yarn/fabric or branded home textiles.
- But the bulk US export flow should be read as garment assembly / textile manufacturing capacity, QIZ duty-free access, and regional supply-chain positioning.
- A product should not be marketed or costed as "Egyptian cotton" unless the supplier can provide fiber-origin proof, yarn/fabric traceability, and buyer-acceptable certification/testing.

### Carpets exported from Egypt to the US
Carpets are one of Egypt's meaningful US-bound non-apparel export categories, but they are separate from furniture. US-reported imports from Egypt under HS 57, carpets and other textile floor coverings, were about USD 139.0 million in 2024 and USD 126.6 million in 2025.

The 2024 US-reported breakdown was:
- woven, not tufted or flocked: USD 90.0 million;
- tufted: USD 37.6 million;
- felt, not tufted: USD 9.6 million;
- knotted: USD 1.1 million;
- other: USD 0.7 million.

Egypt-reported exports to the US show a similar but not identical 2024 total of about USD 123.3 million:
- woven, not tufted or flocked: USD 78.8 million;
- other carpets/floor coverings: USD 19.6 million;
- tufted: USD 18.8 million;
- felt, not tufted: USD 5.5 million;
- knotted: USD 0.6 million.

Material inference:
- The dominant product is likely machine-made synthetic/man-made-fiber pile carpet and floor covering, not handmade wool/silk rugs.
- WITS HS6 data shows Egypt as a large exporter of HS 570232, woven pile floor coverings of man-made textile materials, and a smaller exporter of HS 570242, also man-made textile woven pile floor coverings.
- Knotted carpets, the category closer to traditional hand-knotted rugs, are tiny in the US-bound mix.

Likely use:
- residential and light-commercial floor coverings: area rugs, room rugs, runners, decorative floor coverings, and mass-market machine-made carpets;
- not primarily furniture components, and not mainly premium hand-knotted heritage rugs.

For US imports of higher-end hand-knotted wool/silk-type rugs, the origin-country pattern is different from Egypt's machine-made carpet position:
- HS 570110, knotted carpets of wool or fine animal hair: 2024 US imports were about USD 295.3 million, led by India, Pakistan, Nepal, Turkey, and Morocco.
- HS 570190, knotted carpets of other textile materials, a bucket that can include silk and other non-wool fibers: 2024 US imports were about USD 94.4 million, led by India, Nepal, Turkey, China, and Thailand, with Pakistan, Italy, Vietnam, Egypt, and Morocco much smaller.

Practical reading:
- The US high-end hand-knotted wool rug supply base is mainly India, Pakistan, Nepal, and Turkey.
- Silk or silk-blend hand-knotted rugs are not cleanly isolated in HS6 public tables, but the "other textile materials" knotted category points especially to India and Nepal, with Turkey, China, and Thailand also visible.
- Egypt appears in the knotted "other materials" bucket at about USD 0.59 million in 2024, but this is marginal relative to India and Nepal and should not be treated as a major US high-end hand-knotted rug source.

## Egypt exports to China and Asia

### China
Egypt's exports to China are small relative to US/Arab/EU flows. In 2024, Egypt exports to China were reported around USD 393.7 million. Leading categories:
- edible fruits/nuts/citrus peel/melons;
- vegetable textile fibers / paper yarn / woven fabric;
- mineral fuels/oils;
- salt/sulphur/stone/cement;
- copper;
- ores/slag/ash;
- hides/leather;
- cotton;
- inorganic chemicals;
- stone/plaster/cement/asbestos/mica-type materials.

Furniture to China is not material. Trading Economics showed broad furniture/lighting/prefab exports to China of about USD 2.1 million in 2025, mostly lamps/lighting, with "other furniture" only about USD 146,000.

As of 2026-05-10, Egypt should be treated as included in China's expanded African zero-tariff policy, but only from the effective date forward:
- From 2024-12-01, China granted zero tariff treatment on 100% of tariff lines to least developed countries with diplomatic relations, including 33 African LDCs. Egypt was not in this LDC group.
- From 2026-05-01 to 2028-04-30, China expanded zero-tariff treatment to 20 African non-LDC countries with diplomatic ties. Chinese government/Xinhua reporting explicitly identifies Egypt as one of the relatively better-off countries benefiting from the expanded arrangement.
- For tariff-quota products, only in-quota tariff rates are reduced to zero; out-of-quota rates remain unchanged.

Practical implication:
- Egyptian-origin goods shipped to China after 2026-05-01 may be eligible for preferential zero tariff treatment, subject to origin documentation, product-specific rules, tariff-quota limits where relevant, and customs execution.
- Shipments before 2026-05-01 should not be assumed to have enjoyed this treatment unless they qualified under another preferential basis.

### East and Southeast Asia
The pattern outside China is also not furniture-led:
- Japan: 2025 Egypt exports about USD 57.5 million, led by fruits/nuts, oil seeds, vegetables, animal-feed residues, carpets, electrical equipment, apparel, inorganic chemicals, iron/steel articles, and food preparations.
- South Korea: 2024 imports from Egypt about USD 201.6 million, dominated by mineral fuels/oils and animal-feed residues, with small amounts of apparel, fruit, food preparations, chemicals, and furniture.
- Singapore: 2024 imports from Egypt about USD 92.5 million, dominated by mineral fuels and fruits/nuts, plus apparel, fragrances/cosmetics, food preparations, machinery, and stone/cement materials.
- Thailand: 2024 imports from Egypt about USD 39.3 million, led by sugar/confectionery, pharmaceuticals, electrical equipment, miscellaneous manufactures, vegetables, apparel, aluminum, fertilizers, and fruit.
- India: Egypt exports to India were about USD 641.6 million in 2025, led by stone/cement/minerals, cotton, mineral fuels, fruit/nuts, fertilizers, inorganic chemicals, iron/steel, oil seeds, hides/leather, and special woven/tufted fabrics.

### Practical reading
For furniture and curtain-related manufacturing, East Asia is not currently the demand anchor. It is more likely to be:
- a supplier base into Egypt for furniture inputs, lighting, hardware, machinery, and some finished goods;
- a buyer of Egyptian agricultural/commodity/textile-input exports;
- a limited or niche buyer of Egyptian furniture.

For Egyptian furniture exports, the actionable market logic is regional proximity, Arabic taste fit, shipping cost, and project/custom demand in Gulf/Iraq/Libya/Sudan/UAE, with the US as a smaller apparel-dominated trade relationship.

## Implications for an Egypt manufacturing project
- Furniture export proof does not automatically prove global competitiveness. It proves regional competitiveness around Damietta-style wood furniture and Arab-market channels.
- If the project is curtain-related rather than furniture, the useful analogy is not "Egypt can export furniture to the world"; it is "Egypt can export bulky/home-related products regionally when design, finishing, logistics, and channel fit are right."
- US opportunity should be evaluated separately from furniture: the US buys Egyptian apparel, carpets, food/agri products, minerals/cement, glass, and metals at scale; Egyptian furniture into the US is small.
- China/East Asia should not be treated as a demand market for Egyptian furniture without buyer-level evidence.
- Damietta's cluster strength is a potential sourcing/production-learning asset, especially for woodwork, upholstery, finishing, workshops, and subcontracting networks, but it does not remove the need to validate product-specific channels.
