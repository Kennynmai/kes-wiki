# Bathtub Filter KES Product Architecture Hypotheses

## Why this page exists
This page converts the research into KES-relevant product architecture hypotheses.

These are not recommendations yet. They are structured bets about what sort of bathtub-filter concept might be more or less viable.

## Hypothesis 1 — Do not start with a maximal-claims bath ball
### Reason
- bath-ball competitors often carry broad contaminant storytelling
- review/performance snippets suggest flow and proof tensions
- claim-compliance risk rises quickly

### KES reading
A generic bath-ball copycat route is likely easy to enter but hard to trust.

## Hypothesis 2 — A narrow chlorine-focused route is more defensible than a broad skin-outcome route
### Reason
- chlorine-focused language is the strongest currently surfaced defensible zone
- Sprite-style narrower media story appears more believable than Tubo/Kinder-style broad outcome claims

### KES reading
If KES explores this category, it likely should prefer:
- chlorine / odor / bath-comfort framing
over
- eczema / baby-safety / skin-outcome framing

## Hypothesis 3 — Native tub-spout compatibility is a core product problem, not a minor accessory issue
### Reason
- installation, slip, fit, and flow tradeoffs appear central
- “universal fit” is likely overstated market-wide

### KES reading
A viable product may need compatibility discipline, adapter logic, or a more explicit faucet-fit scope rather than vague universality.

## Hypothesis 4 — Premium visual design can justify price, but only if performance is not obviously weak
### Reason
- Canopy proves premium bath-filter positioning is possible
- reviewer content also shows that premium design invites harder performance scrutiny

### KES reading
A KES offer could borrow premium visual codes, but would need a stronger practical proof story than aesthetics alone.

## Hypothesis 5 — A soft-hanging / ritual-oriented route may reduce hardware intimidation but increase maintenance friction
### Reason
- Santevia-style hanging filter appears easier to frame as wellness and skin comfort
- but drying and short replacement cadence add friction

### KES reading
This route might be attractive if KES wants to avoid hard-plastic hardware aesthetics, but should be evaluated against user tolerance for ritual maintenance.

## Hypothesis 6 — The most credible winner may be a hybrid: premium-design + narrower technical promise + realistic flow discipline
### Reason
Current research suggests the strongest combination may be:
- less aggressive claim stack
- better perceived bathroom fit/aesthetics
- realistic install story
- explicit maintenance logic
- no medicalized promise

### KES reading
This appears more promising than either extreme:
- pure commodity bath-ball clone
- pure eczema/baby-outcome DTC hype product

## Open design questions for KES
- native tub-spout vs shower-to-bath workaround?
- narrow chlorine comfort story vs broader filtration story?
- cartridge economics that feel acceptable at bath usage levels?
- family/baby adjacency without safety overclaim?
- premium-soft visual identity vs technical-trust identity vs hybrid?
