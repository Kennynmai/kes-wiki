# Bathtub Filter Brand Operating Matrix

## Purpose
Compare leading bathtub-filter brands on product form, technical route, core claims, believable value, likely friction, and likely claim/compliance risk.

## Matrix
| Brand | Product form | Likely route type | Strongest visible claim | Most believable value | Likely friction | Likely compliance risk | Channel identity |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Santevia | soft hanging bath filter / organic-cotton style | bath-faucet hanging / media-contact | chlorine reduction + sensitive-skin comfort | better chlorine reduction story at realistic flow than many peers; lifestyle differentiation | short replacement cadence (about 2 months), drying/maintenance burden | medium | premium wellness / family lifestyle |
| Sprite | Bath Pure / bath-ball-adjacent + shower lineage | shower-to-bath extension / patented media route | patented Chlorgon media removes free chlorine, dirt, odors | stronger chlorine-focused technical credibility than many DTC brands | fit / attachment / housing issues by analogy from shower reviews; bath cartridge rated only ~25-30 baths | medium | utility + legacy filtration brand |
| Crystal Quest | hard-shell bath ball | bath-ball route | broad contaminant list + pH balance + softer skin/hair | established bath-ball form factor, visible replacement ecosystem | flow sensitivity, flimsy-feeling design in review snippets, broad-claim stack | medium-high | marketplace + filtration brand |
| Canopy | premium silicone-like bathtub filter | premium DTC bath-spout route | baby bath / gentler bath / reduce chlorine and irritants | strong design language and baby-channel fit | premium price, slower-flow performance criticism, “tested to” vs certified confusion | medium-high | premium DTC / baby registry / wellness |
| Kinder Filter | bath filter for sensitive skin families | DTC bath-ball / child-sensitive-skin route | clinically tested / improve skin hydration / eczema-oriented | very strong parent/sensitive-skin emotional conversion | aggressive skin-outcome wording, evidence boundary risk | high | DTC baby / sensitive-skin |
| Tubo | DTC bathtub filter | bath-faucet attachment route | removes chlorine / contaminants / bacteria and viruses | strong easy-install, family, eczema-friendly conversion framing | very aggressive contaminant/health claims, replacement economics uncertain | very high | DTC social / parent problem-solving |

## Notes by brand
### Santevia
- strongest visible commercial differentiator is not broad contaminant bravado, but softer premium/lifestyle framing plus better third-party review performance at faster flow
- downside is unusually short replacement cadence

### Sprite
- strongest tie between public marketing and a visible technical/patent story
- more chlorine-focused and narrower than some broad-claim competitors, which may actually be an advantage

### Crystal Quest
- category-defining for bath-ball form factor
- useful benchmark for the broad-promise version of the category

### Canopy
- shows how the category can be repositioned into design-led premium baby/wellness territory
- useful benchmark for price/design premium vs performance skepticism

### Kinder / Tubo
- commercially interesting because they reveal how far the category can be pushed into eczema/child-sensitive-skin conversion
- also most useful for future page-level compliance audits
