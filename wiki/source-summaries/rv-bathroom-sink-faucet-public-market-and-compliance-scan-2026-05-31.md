## RV Market Signals
- RVIA Winter 2025 RoadSigns forecast projected 2026 wholesale shipments in the 332,100-366,000 unit range, with a 349,300 median.
- RVIA March 2026 report showed March wholesale shipments at 32,162, down 13.9% year-over-year, with first-quarter shipments down 12.1%. Towables remained the majority of shipments; motorhomes were up year-to-date.
- Interpretation: new RV shipment context is mixed in early 2026, but the installed base and replacement/repair logic remain important for a faucet replacement SKU.

## Compliance Signals
- DOE / eCFR federal standards list lavatory faucet maximum water use at 2.2 gpm at 60 psi.
- EPA WaterSense bathroom faucet guidance describes WaterSense-labeled bathroom sink faucets/accessories historically using max 1.5 gpm and independent certification; EPA paused draft Version 2.0 work in February 2025 after proposing 1.2 gpm.
- NSF consumer guidance says kitchen and bathroom faucets sold in the US should meet NSF/ANSI/CAN 61 lead leaching requirements; lead-free claims also implicate NSF/ANSI/CAN 372.
- ASME A112.18.1 / CSA B125.1 covers plumbing supply fittings including kitchen, sink, and lavatory supply fittings.

## Extracted Implications For KES
- The "metal RV faucet" idea already exists, so KES cannot rely on metal alone as novelty.
- The opportunity is sharper: metal-critical design plus RV-specific fit proof, leak proof, and evidence-backed claims.
- Competitor pages show that dimension details such as shank length and thread size are commercially relevant and should be included in KES page architecture.
- Certification language must be SKU-specific and evidence-backed.
