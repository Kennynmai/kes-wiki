# Raw Capture — Bathtub Filter Brand / Patent / Product-Line / Content Scouting (2026-04-12)

## Purpose
Extend the bathtub-filter topic beyond generic patent and evidence mapping into:
- major brand/product-line reconnaissance
- brand-linked patent/technology posture clues
- SNS / blogger / UGC-style content signals
- category-coverage gap checking

## Brand and product-line signals seen so far
### Sprite / Sprite Showers
- repeated public positioning around **patented Chlorgon / Chloragon-type media**
- brand appears stronger in shower filters but repeatedly extends the same filtration logic into bath ball / bath-ball-adjacent use
- multiple reseller pages explicitly connect bath-ball products to:
  - chlorine reduction
  - sensitive skin / itchy skin / eczema-adjacent language
  - hot-water suitability compared with generic media
- implication:
  - strong example of bathtub category being inherited from shower-filter brand equity rather than built as a standalone category from scratch

### Crystal Quest
- bath-ball format appears core to visible bathtub-filter presence
- public pages / reseller pages emphasize:
  - chlorine and chloramine reduction
  - broad contaminant lists
  - pH-balancing / softer-skin language
- Justia surfaced a **Bath ball filter** design-patent result (`D1034899`) that appears category-relevant and may be worth deeper checking later
- implication:
  - Crystal Quest seems important for product-form and marketing-language analysis, especially around bath-ball format and broad contaminant storytelling

### Santevia
- more differentiated visual/product story: organic cotton bath faucet filter and mineral-adding / plastic-light narrative
- recurring market/review language emphasizes:
  - chlorine removal
  - sensitive skin / eczema-prone user relevance
  - mineral addition / “nourishing” angle
- implication:
  - useful counterexample to hard-plastic bath-ball format; relevant for premium / lifestyle / sustainability positioning analysis

### Kinder / baby-sensitive-skin angle
- surfaced as a strong **eczema / baby / sensitive-skin** commercial framing example
- deserves later claim-discipline review because public-facing language appears more aggressive than the current evidence base would comfortably support

## Public content layer signals
### Review/publisher layer
- WaterFilterGuru appears highly relevant because it is already testing bath filters comparatively and discussing flow-rate vs chlorine-reduction performance
- Parents.com surfaced a mainstream-parenting framing that bath water filters may matter for sensitive skin / eczema contexts
- Interior Medicine surfaced a more explanatory angle: chlorine may matter, but hard water also matters

### Blog/reseller layer
- multiple reseller/blog pages repeat a similar persuasive formula:
  - chlorine causes dry/itchy skin
  - filter helps babies / children / eczema-prone users
  - bath-ball or faucet-hang format is renter-friendly
- this layer is important because it likely shapes category conversion language more than academic papers do

### Community / UGC layer
- Reddit surfaced repeatedly in bath-filter / eczema / granola-parent contexts
- signal pattern is mixed:
  - some users report chlorine-smell reduction and softer-feeling water
  - some users are skeptical of eczema-specific benefit claims
  - some users suggest simple mitigation routines or emollient use instead
- implication:
  - UGC appears useful not just for support, but for identifying where the category’s credibility breaks down

## Early strategic interpretation
The category is now showing at least three distinct content ecosystems:
1. **technology/filtration brands** (Sprite, Crystal Quest)
2. **lifestyle / premium wellness framing** (Santevia and similar)
3. **parent / sensitive-skin / eczema problem-solving discourse** (Parents.com, Reddit, mom/community spaces, reseller blogs)
