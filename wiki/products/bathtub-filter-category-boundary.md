## Why this page exists
Bathtub filter appears to be a fuzzy category. This page clarifies what it is, what it is adjacent to, and what it should not be confused with.

## Working definition
A bathtub filter is a bathing-use water-treatment accessory designed to reduce or improve undesirable characteristics of tub-fill water, usually through a temporary or semi-temporary attachment rather than permanent plumbing.

## Adjacent categories
### Shower filter
Relationship:
- closest adjacent category
- often more mature and better known
- sometimes used as a substitute solution for filling a tub via hose / handheld setup

Implication:
- bathtub filter may be better understood as an extension of shower-filter intent rather than a fully standalone top-level category

### Bath ball filter
Relationship:
- likely a major sub-form inside bathtub filter
- sometimes used almost interchangeably with bathtub filter in consumer-facing language

Implication:
- not every bathtub filter is a bath ball, but bath ball may be one of the most recognized physical forms in this niche

### Whole-house filtration
Relationship:
- often framed as the more complete solution
- bathtub filter positioned as easier, cheaper, lower-friction alternative

Implication:
- bathtub filter is often a compromise or convenience category, not the “ultimate” filtration solution

### Faucet / sink filters
Relationship:
- overlap in attachment logic but not in use case
- bathtub filter is sold around bathing comfort and sensitive users, not drinking or washing produce

## What the category is not
- not a permanent plumbing filtration system
- not necessarily a true water softener in the strongest technical sense
- not inherently a medically validated eczema solution
- not the same as generic bath additives or soak products

## Current category read
The strongest current read is:
- category exists
- category language is fuzzy
- category is demand-led by use case, not by engineering taxonomy

In practice, consumers appear to understand it through questions like:
- will this make bath water gentler for my baby?
- will this reduce chlorine smell?
- will this help sensitive skin?
- can I use this easily in a rental or apartment?

## Why this matters for KES
If KES explores the category, positioning should avoid pretending the category is more standardized or more scientifically resolved than it currently is.

Instead, KES should decide whether it wants to frame the category primarily as:
- baby/sensitive-skin bath accessory
- chlorine-reduction comfort accessory
- rental-friendly workaround product
- premium bathing upgrade
