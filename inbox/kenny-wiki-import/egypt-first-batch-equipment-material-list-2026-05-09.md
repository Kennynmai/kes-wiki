# Egypt First-Batch Equipment Material List - 2026-05-09

## Why this source matters

This is the first concrete equipment/material import list for the Egypt curtain project. It turns the manufacturing plan from a general "curtain factory" idea into a specific first-batch capability set, with equipment names, specs, HS codes where known, packaging form, quantity, use, and purchase-cost text.

## Key points

- The list covers 20 text rows. Embedded images were intentionally not saved.
- Production scope stated in the sheet: roller blinds, zebra / soft gauze blinds, Shangri-La blinds, honeycomb blinds, "厂字型" Venetian blinds, and electric opening-curtain tracks.
- First-batch strategy stated in the sheet: keep equipment and product scope lean; add other products/equipment only after customers stabilize and the sales network is established.
- Rows with HS codes: 5.
- Nominal text subtotal using the first visible numeric purchase amount in each row: RMB 218,961.
- If the vibrating blade cutter is modeled at the bargained RMB 130,000 instead of the listed RMB 148,000, subtotal becomes RMB 200,961, before unresolved tax, packaging, and transport effects.
- Footer budget target: control total budget within RMB 200,000.

## Equipment groups

- Fabric cutting: vibrating blade fabric cutter.
- Venetian-blind forming/cutting/punching: full automatic Venetian blind machine.
- Compressed-air system: screw air compressor, refrigerated air dryer, air tank.
- Rail punching/cutting/bending: honeycomb rail puncher, Venetian rail puncher, day-night blind cutting/drilling machine, electric rail bending machine.
- Setup and workshop tools: welding machine, cutting machine, aluminum saw, lift, drill press, impact drill, hand drill, ladders.
- Labeling/coding: inkjet printer, label printer.

## Caveats / tensions

- The stated budget target conflicts with the nominal subtotal: RMB 218,961 using listed text amounts, or RMB 200,961 if one unaccepted bargain price is substituted.
- The largest item explicitly excludes tax, packaging, and transport, so final landed procurement cost may exceed the sheet subtotal.
- HS codes are incomplete. Only the first five rows have HS codes.
- Some tools have no package form or HS code, which may matter for packing list, customs classification, and shipment documentation.
- This is not yet an import-ready packing/commercial invoice. It is a planning list.

## Suggested wiki updates

- Use this list as the initial equipment baseline for import-readiness, ACI/NAFEZA, HS-code pre-check, packing-list preparation, and factory layout.
- Before shipment, reconcile equipment dimensions, power requirements, packaging, HS codes, and whether each item is equipment, tool, spare part, or accessory.
