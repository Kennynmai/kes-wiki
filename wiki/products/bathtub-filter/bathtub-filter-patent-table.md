# 浴缸过滤器（bathtub filter）专利表

## 为什么有这页
这页把 bathtub filter 相关公开专利线索整理成更可比较的工作表。

它**不是**法律意义上的 FTO（freedom to operate）意见，
而是一份用于比较：
- route type（路线类型）
- technical signal（技术信号）
- brand adjacency（与现有品牌路线的邻接）
- claim implication（对宣称的含义）
- 与 KES 的潜在相关性

的战略研究工具。

## Brand-level 公开可见判断

### Sprite
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**强**
- 更像绑定 `US5914043A` / `US6056875A` 所代表的 calcium sulfite / hot-water dechlorination lineage
- `US6325930B2` 文本还直接引用 ChlorgonTM
- 结论：Sprite 更像 **media-IP-led**，不是单纯 bath-ball 卖家

### Crystal Quest
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**中等偏弱**
- Bath Ball® 页面前台更强调 unique drop-down filter design、more contact time、chloramine removal ceramic spheres
- 暂未快速看到其 Bath Ball® 与单一强 patent family 的明确公开绑定
- 结论：更像 **form-factor + cartridge configuration** 路线

### Santevia
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**弱到中**
- 前台更强调 chlorine reduction、restores minerals、skin & hair comfort、hanging / reusable ritual
- 暂未快速看到像 Sprite 一样的前台 patent anchor
- 结论：更像 **wellness / mineralization / hanging accessory** 路线

### Canopy
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**弱到中**
- 前台更强调 design、baby extension、chlorine / odor reduction、replaceable cartridge
- 暂未快速看到明确 brand-owned bath-filter patent 在前台高频展示
- 结论：更像 **design-first inline cartridge** 路线

### Kinder Filter
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**弱**
- 前台主打 clinically tested、skin hydration、eczema / dry skin 等结果表达
- 暂未快速看到明确 bath-filter patent family 与之公开绑定
- 结论：更像 **clinical-positioning DTC** 路线

### Tubo
- 公开可见专利 / 技术关联：**弱**
- 前台强调 8-stage、99% chlorine / heavy metals / bacteria、clinically tested
- 暂未快速看到明确公开专利锚点
- 结论：更像 **performance-claim-heavy marketplace / DTC** 路线

## 2015–2025 新增值得关注的专利条目

| Patent | 标题 | 路线类型 | 信息 |
|---|---|---|---|
| `D1,034,899` | Bath Ball Filter | 外观设计专利 | 2024-07-09 授权；受让人未完全确认；非 utility patent，不保护功能机制 |

**整体新增 utility patent 活动（2015–2025）：极少。**
DTC 新品牌（Canopy、Kinder、Tubo）公开可见层面均无功能型专利保护。Canopy 可能持有至少一项设计专利申请，内容未完全确认。

## 当前最值得 KES 内部记住的三条
1. **最强 prior art 压力**：tub-spout inline + shower-lineage cartridge（均属旧专利，大多临近或已过期）
2. **最像真实技术护城河的公开品牌线索**：Sprite / Chlorgon 的 hot-water dechlorination media lineage
3. **DTC 新品牌没有明显专利保护**：Canopy / Kinder / Tubo 的护城河更多是品牌和渠道，而非 IP
4. **最需要守住 claim discipline 的区域**：softening、broad contaminant removal、eczema / baby outcome claims

## 不要过度解读的地方
- 没快速搜到 brand patent，不等于 brand 没有申请
- 某 route 有老专利，不等于今天就不可做
- 某品牌写“patented”或“clinically tested”，也不等于所有销售文案都被同等级证据覆盖

**Implication: prior-art pressure on KES is lower than the original table suggested.** The classic tub-spout inline + shower-cartridge architectures are in the public domain. KES can freely adopt those form-factor conventions.

### The "Sprite Chlorgon lineage" is actually the Farley family lineage

Every major Sprite-adjacent patent is personally owned by David K. Farley or family members (Michelle Farley, David Aaron Farley), not by Sprite Industries as a corporate entity. The `US6325930B2` text citing "ChlorgonTM" is self-citation — the Farley patent family cites the Farley invention.

This matters because:
- Sprite Industries' "moat" is not corporate IP; it is a single inventor's personal portfolio licensed to the company.
- If Farley stops prosecuting, the lineage dies.
- Any KES licensing conversation would be with an individual, not a company legal team.

### Revised KES-facing read

1. **Prior-art pressure from old patents is effectively zero.** Every patent on the original table is expired, lapsed, or withdrawn. Tub-spout inline + shower-cartridge architecture is free to use.
2. **The real live IP is Farley (personal), Homewerks (corporate), and FilterBaby (corporate, aggressive).** KES needs to route-avoid these three specifically.
3. **FilterBaby's `US12534389B2` is the single most important patent to read and design around.** The "skin-treatment adapter" and "UV sterilization" claims potentially cover the entire DTC skincare-filter category. KES should read the full claim set and identify non-infringing variants before committing to a skincare-positioned bath SKU.
4. **No visible utility patent held by Canopy, Kinder Filter, or Tubo.** Their moats remain brand/segment/channel, consistent with the original IP-depth map. Kinder's "patented advanced filtration" language on the website is **not corroborated** by a visible filing under that brand.
5. **Chlorgon is a marketing mark, not a technology trademark the KES team should fear.** The underlying patents are expired. Trademark status could not be verified in this pass (TSDR blocked for scripted access — see follow-up below).
