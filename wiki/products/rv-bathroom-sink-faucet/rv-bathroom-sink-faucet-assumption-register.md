## Why this page exists
This page tracks the assumptions behind the current conditional GO.

If a critical assumption fails, the go/no-go memo should be updated.

|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A-01 | Users will pay a premium for less leak risk and better material feel | Critical | medium | Price test, competitor review, launch conversion test | Premium route may fail; retreat to lower-cost hybrid or archive |

| A-06 | Extended metal shanks reduce installation complaints without creating new fit issues | Critical | medium-low | Fit bench across vanity thicknesses | Adapter kit or different shank architecture needed |
| A-07 | Spout reach can be improved without increasing splash in small RV basins | High | medium | Basin landing test | High-arc value proposition weakens |
| A-08 | KES can obtain SKU-level certification evidence for desired claims | Critical | medium | Compliance mapping | Listing copy and channel access constrained |
| A-09 | Target price can stay below $49.99 with acceptable margin | Critical | low-medium | BOM / landed cost / FBA model | Route may need simplification |
| A-10 | RV-specific positioning beats generic residential faucet repackaging | High | medium | Sample comparison + page testing | Residential benchmark may become stronger path |

## Assumption Review Rules
- Critical assumptions must be reviewed after sample teardown.
- Any failed critical assumption triggers an update to the go/no-go memo.
- Do not close an assumption based only on page copy; physical sample or hard evidence is required where applicable.
