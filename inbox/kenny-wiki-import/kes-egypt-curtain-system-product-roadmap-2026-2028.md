# KES Egypt Curtain-System Product Roadmap 2026-2028

## Classification

- Type: internal strategy roadmap / product-development plan.
- Context: converts the KES Egypt "curtain-system supply-chain standardization" thesis into a 2026-2028 product, supply-chain, and channel roadmap.
- Verification: unverified. Treat as planning intent until supported by customer orders, technical tests, supplier readiness, and local operating capacity.

## Why this source matters

This note makes the KES Egypt strategy more operational. It defines the company as a standardized curtain-system supply platform for installers, curtain shops, and projects, not simply as a curtain factory. It also names the first product families, test requirements, channel buildout, and localization sequence.

## Core thesis

KES Egypt should not compete primarily on low price. It should build standardized products, standardized supply chains, and standardized installation systems that allow installers, curtain shops, and project customers to receive stable, efficient, repeatable curtain-system solutions.

Strategic line:

> KES Egypt standardizes curtain-system supply for installers, curtain shops and projects.

## Market assumptions

The roadmap assumes:

- Egypt's population and real-estate development create long-term demand for curtain and shading systems.
- Priority regions include New Cairo, the New Administrative Capital, Sheikh Zayed, and Alexandria.
- Demand sources include residential, office, hotel, medical, education, and commercial projects.
- Current supply-chain gaps include inconsistent product standards, messy accessory specifications, unstable delivery cycles, weak installation standards, and limited project execution capability.
- Professional customers value stable products, complete accessories, on-time delivery, and fast after-sales response more than the absolute lowest price.

These assumptions are plausible but still need quantified validation by customer segment.

## Product-development principles

### 1. Standardization first

Product development should prioritize:

- standard sizes;
- standard packaging;
- standard accessory packs;
- standard installation methods.

The goal is lower inventory complexity, higher production efficiency, and better delivery reliability.

### 2. Systemization first

KES should sell complete systems rather than isolated items. A roller blind system, for example, should connect fabric, aluminum tube, brackets, chain, bottom bar, and installation accessories into a coherent SKU structure.

### 3. Mid-to-high-end positioning

KES should avoid low-end price wars and focus on offices, commercial spaces, hotels, and mid-to-high-end residential customers where quality, service, documentation, and delivery reliability matter.

## Roadmap

### 2026: establish the standardized product system

Priority A products:

- blackout roller blinds for offices, schools, hospitals, and hotels;
- sunscreen / screen roller blinds for commercial spaces and office buildings;
- motorized roller blinds for high-end residences, villas, and hotel projects;
- honeycomb blinds for energy-saving buildings and high-end residences.

Required tests:

- high-temperature testing;
- UV aging testing;
- chain lifespan testing;
- installation testing.

### 2027: expand into complete window-treatment systems

Add:

- motorized track systems;
- venetian / blind systems;
- project-grade track systems;
- smart control systems.

This stage moves KES from first product families toward a fuller curtain and shading system offer.

### 2028: build the installer channel system

Build:

- standard product catalog;
- installer training system;
- sample system;
- regional dealer system.

The intended result is an Egypt-wide curtain-system supply network.

## Supply-chain sequence

Initial stage:

- use Chinese supply chains for core components such as fabric, motors, aluminum tubes, and accessories.

Middle stage:

- localize production of rails, brackets, plastic accessories, and other suitable components to reduce cost and improve delivery.

Long term:

- build an Egypt manufacturing center serving Egypt, the Middle East, and North Africa.

## Marketing and channel strategy

Online:

- Facebook;
- Instagram;
- TikTok;
- WhatsApp Business.

Offline:

- installers;
- curtain shops;
- project customers;
- real-estate developers.

The channel model is consistent with earlier notes that installer adoption and B2B reorder behavior should become strategic metrics.

## Implications for KES

1. The first product roadmap is broader than the earlier "under 20 SKUs / first six months" caution, so SKU discipline remains a live constraint.
2. The first 2026 product families should be converted into exact SKU architecture: sizes, fabrics, tubes, brackets, motors, colors, pack contents, and test standards.
3. Product test requirements should become launch gates, especially because Egypt's heat and UV exposure can create early failures.
4. "Hong Kong Design / Egypt Manufacturing / Local Service / Fast Delivery" can become brand architecture only if each claim has visible operational proof.
5. The 2028 installer-channel goal should be prototyped earlier through a small pilot installer program rather than deferred entirely.

## Caveats / tensions

- The roadmap is directionally coherent but still aspirational.
- Product breadth may exceed first-year operational capacity unless the SKU system is tightly controlled.
- The plan assumes mid-to-high-end customers and professional installers will pay for reliability; this needs field proof through quotes, orders, and reorder behavior.
- Localized production of rails, brackets, and plastic parts needs tooling, QC, supplier, and volume validation before becoming a cost advantage.
- Smart control and motorized systems carry higher after-sales risk than manual blinds and should be gated by installer testing.
