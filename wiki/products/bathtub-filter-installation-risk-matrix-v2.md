# Bathtub Filter Installation Risk Matrix — V2

| Risk area | Normalized category | Source-backed signal | Quote anchor | Risk level | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Native tub-spout compatibility | geometry/fit | multiple surfaced pages rely on “fits most tubs” language, implying variance is real | Crystal Quest: “Includes both standard and flexible handles for easy fit” | high | products are solving around real bathtub hardware variation |
| Fill-rate penalty | operating performance | strongest surfaced review theme across reviewer content | Canopy / WaterFilterGuru: “unable to effectively remove chlorine at a ‘normal’ faster faucet flow rate”; Crystal Quest: “couldn’t even remove 100% chlorine at an impractically slow faucet flow rate” | high | users do not want a bathtub to fill slowly just to preserve filter efficacy |
| Slip / wobble / weak attachment | attachment stability | surfaced in marketplace/community commentary | earlier Amazon/community signals referenced slipping off faucet | high | unstable attachment can destroy perceived quality immediately |
| Leak / seam failure | housing integrity | strongly surfaced from shower-filter adjacent reviews and should transfer as a risk heuristic | repeated shower review snippets mention housing/seam leaks | high | bathroom water products get no forgiveness for leakage |
| Drying / care burden | maintenance burden | especially relevant to soft-hanging route | Santevia: “Simply hang it to dry in between uses. Do not wring it out.” | medium | adds ritual friction and possible hygiene concerns |
| Universal-fit overclaim | marketing risk | broad category tendency rather than one isolated brand | many pages imply universal or easy fit without strong compatibility schema | high | likely a mismatch point between ad promise and real-home reality |
