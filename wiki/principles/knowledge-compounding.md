# Knowledge Compounding

## Principle statement
Important inputs and high-value answers should not disappear into chat history. They should improve the system that future thinking depends on.

## Why it exists
A knowledge system becomes powerful when it compounds:
- sources improve topics
- topics improve syntheses
- syntheses improve principles and protocols
- decisions become more traceable and less reactive

## When it applies
- repeated questions
- durable topics
- workflows being refined over time
- decisions that benefit from historical reasoning

## When it may fail
- when the system captures too much low-value material
- when write-back becomes bureaucracy
- when pages are created without durable purpose
