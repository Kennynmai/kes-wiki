# KES Egypt Truthful Specification and Standardization

## Principle statement

KES Egypt should make truthful specifications, truthful materials, and truthful promotion a company operating principle. Parameters can be conservative, but they must not be inflated.

## Why it exists

In a market where imported products may be associated with exaggerated claims, truthful product data can become a trust asset. The strategic goal is to move KES away from "Chinese products sold into Egypt" and toward "Egypt local standardized manufacturing."

If Egypt's market becomes more standards-driven over time, the companies that survive should depend less on information gaps, relationships, and price gaps, and more on standards, quality, delivery, and brand.

## Operating rules

### 1. Conservative parameters

State performance claims below tested capability when appropriate.

Example: if a curtain rail can actually carry 35 kg under defined test conditions, public materials should claim `<=30 kg`, not 40 kg.

### 2. SKU standard sheets

Every SKU should have a product standard sheet before broad promotion:

- product code;
- material specification;
- aluminum thickness;
- surface treatment;
- accessory material;
- packaging standard;
- load-bearing standard;
- installation instructions;
- batch / supplier record.

### 3. Supplier inspection

Supplier relationship is not a substitute for inspection. Incoming and random checks should cover high-risk attributes:

- aluminum wall thickness;
- weight;
- dimensional tolerance;
- plastic-part toughness;
- load-bearing and moving-part performance;
- material identity when a grade is claimed.

### 4. No unsupported superlatives

Marketing, website, marketplace listings, brochures, and quotation sheets should avoid unsupported claims such as:

- "Egypt's first brand";
- "the best curtain system";
- "lifetime no failure";
- material or performance claims without test or purchase evidence.

Prefer proof-bearing language:

- local production / assembly;
- standardized supply;
- tested load-bearing data;
- traceable batch management;
- clear installation standard;
- local spare parts and support.

## When it applies

- product development;
- supplier selection;
- QC and incoming inspection;
- SKU launch;
- website and brochure writing;
- marketplace listing claims;
- installer training materials;
- project quotations and spec sheets.

## When it may fail

- competitors inflate specifications and create short-term pressure to match them;
- KES lacks testing tools or consistent inspection discipline;
- sales materials are created before product standard sheets exist;
- claims are copied from suppliers without verification;
- batch records are not maintained after first launch.
