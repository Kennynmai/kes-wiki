# Bathtub Filter Product Definition Language

## Purpose
Translate research into language KES could eventually use internally to define a viable product route.

## What KES can probably say
- chlorine-conscious bath-water comfort
- gentler bath experience
- designed for realistic bath use
- replaceable filtration media
- premium bath-time upgrade
- family bath-water comfort

## What KES should say only with strong proof
- removes free chlorine by X% under stated conditions
- compatible with these specific tub-spout formats
- tested under real bath-fill conditions

## What KES should avoid saying
- improves eczema
- baby-safe because it removes harmful chemicals
- softens hard water in a clinically meaningful way
- removes a broad list of contaminants without route-specific proof
- clinically proven skin improvement

## Likely target user language
### Strongest plausible audiences
- chlorine-conscious bath users
- families who want gentler bath water
- premium bathroom/wellness buyers
- sensitive-skin households (without promising medical outcome)

### Less safe audience language
- eczema cure-seeking buyers
- baby medical-safety framing
- toxin-panic maximalists

## Working definition candidate
A bathtub filter for KES would most plausibly be defined as:

> a premium-but-disciplined bath-water comfort product that aims to reduce chlorine-related irritation burden under realistic bath-use conditions, without overpromising medical or broad contaminant outcomes.
