# Bathtub Filter — Complaint Taxonomy by Route Notes (2026-04-13)

## Purpose
Convert the growing set of review and marketplace complaint signals into a route-based risk taxonomy that is more useful for product screening.

## Current signal inputs
- editorial review/testing pages already captured in `2026-04-13-market-review-pass`
- marketplace-negative-review snippet notes in `2026-04-13-marketplace-negative-review-signals.md`
- route structure from product-form, installation-risk, and brand-operating pages

## Route buckets
### 1. Bath-ball / hard-shell universal route
Representative examples:
- Crystal Quest bath ball
- generic Amazon-compatible bath-ball / tub-faucet filters

Recurring complaint/risk patterns:
- slips off faucet
- compatibility instability despite “fits most tubs” language
- broad claim stack outruns user-perceived benefit
- chlorine-removal disputes
- premium price can feel unjustified if efficacy is ambiguous

### 2. Soft-hanging / fabric-like route
Representative examples:
- Santevia bath filter

Recurring complaint/risk patterns:
- slower fill expectation or fill-speed misunderstanding
- overflow in some real-use or seller-quality contexts
- drying / maintenance burden
- replacement cadence may feel short

### 3. Faucet-attach / forced-path route
Representative examples:
- premium spout-attach concepts like Canopy
- generic faucet-attach tub filters

Recurring complaint/risk patterns:
- realistic normal-flow efficacy skepticism
- fit complexity across tub-spout geometries
- leak / overflow / operating awkwardness if water path is not stable
- higher price intensifies review scrutiny

### 4. Sensitive-skin / baby / eczema-forward route
Representative examples:
- Canopy baby-oriented positioning
- Kinder / Tubo style sensitive-skin conversion routes

Recurring complaint/risk patterns:
- strongest expectation mismatch if benefit is not obvious
- claim-sensitive language can amplify disappointment into distrust
- any fit or performance issue becomes worse because the emotional promise is stronger

## Cross-route complaint classes
### Efficacy doubt
- doesn’t remove chlorine
- doesn’t work as advertised
- no obvious difference in skin / comfort

### Fit failure
- slips off faucet
- curved faucet mismatch
- not as universal as implied

### Operating frustration
- overflow
- too slow to fill tub
- awkward tuning / positioning needed

### Value disappointment
- too expensive for what it does
- refill cadence or replacement burden feels too high

## Working interpretation
The category should be screened not only by brand and claim, but by **route-specific failure mode**.
A route can look commercially attractive and still be structurally fragile if its natural complaint class is too severe.
