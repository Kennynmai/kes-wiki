# KES flat silicone strap spout-fit design note

Date: 2026-06-03

## Scope

This folder archives user-provided KES attachment design notes and render images for bathtub-filter spout-fit coverage.

## Design Notes From User

- Main hanger is a flat silicone strap, 21 mm wide.
- The left and right strap sides each have 4 adjustment holes to tune strap length.
- For tub spouts with a pull-up diverter, the center of the strap has a circular hanging hole that can stretch over the diverter pull knob.
- The flat strap differs from competitor round cord hangers: the wider contact area against the bathtub spout should reduce swing under high-flow / high-impact tub-fill conditions.
- Center-hole diverter mounting should keep the filter centered under the outlet, reducing side impact and water missing the filter body.
- For non-diverter tub spouts, KES proposes an included 3M adhesive hook attached to the tub spout to simulate the hanging point.
- For curved / irregular spouts, KES proposes an included 5-hole silicone tie, 124 mm total length and 20 mm width, to secure the 3M adhesive hook.
- The primary retention force is the silicone tie's tension and friction against the spout. The 3M adhesive is not the primary load-bearing mechanism; its role is an auxiliary anti-slip layer so the hard ABS hook does not slide against the metal tub spout contact surface.
- Wall-distance boundary was corrected on 2026-06-17: outlet center-to-wall distance >= 60 mm is the centered/aesthetic line, not a functional support limit.
- The 60 mm centered line comes from the current KES product envelope: 120 mm / 2 = 60 mm.
- KES can tolerate about 20 mm off-center installation, so outlet center-to-wall distance 40-60 mm is usable with an aesthetic compromise. User observation: typical bathtub spouts are not under 40 mm from wall, and competitor review evidence does not show concentrated complaints around this clearance point.

## Evidence Boundary

Covered as design intent:

- straight spout with pull-up diverter knob
- straight non-diverter spout using adhesive hook
- curved / irregular spout using adhesive hook plus short tie
- competitor differentiation vs. round cord hanging

Still needs physical validation:

- silicone tie tension / friction after hot bath-fill conditions
- ABS hook displacement or rotation while compressed by the silicone tie
- silicone hole stretch limits across real diverter knob diameters
- swing / retention under 18-25 L/min and 30-35 L/min flow
- bypass / side-impact water under off-center spouts
- compatibility with short projection and low wall clearance edge cases, especially outlet center-to-wall distance 40-60 mm with off-center installation
- confirming splash / bypass / visual acceptance across 40-60 mm off-center installs, and centered fit when outlet center-to-wall distance >= 60 mm
- compatibility with wide-body decorative spouts and wobbling slip-fit spouts
