# Egypt Company Progress Meeting - 2026-05-05

## Classification
- Type: meeting-note / project-status discussion.

## One-line summary
The local Egypt execution view is that renting a suitable factory is the next gating step: after lease, the company should change from trading-only to production + trading, start NAFEZA/customs/import preparation, renovate for roughly two months, and later apply for temporary then formal IDA production licensing.

## Current claimed status
- EG Bank account reportedly opened on April 16.
- Prior intermediary relationship reportedly ended, with documents obtained around April 10.
- Company charter/articles, lease contract, seal, and core materials reportedly handed over by April 18.
- Work-visa process reportedly submitted April 19; estimated remaining path about two weeks after investor certificate.
- Accounting/tax/audit provider selected; annual fee reportedly 46,000 EGP, to start after payment.
- Trademark registration planned for 5 categories at 5,000 EGP each, total 25,000 EGP.

## Main proposed execution sequence
1. Rent factory first.
2. Use factory lease/address to change company business scope from trading-only to production + trading.
3. Put factory lease conditions into the lease contract, including landlord-provided industry permission, building/construction acceptance, fire-related documents, and other documents required by the lawyer/intermediary.
4. During the change period, start factory renovation and utility/fire/electrical work.
5. After company change, register or activate NAFEZA/customs path, establish equipment-import approval file, obtain customs release letter / ACID, and coordinate China-side export documents and CargoX filing.
6. After equipment arrives at port and is delivered to the factory, apply to IDA for temporary production permission/license.
7. Then process formal production license, with claimed period around 6 months.

## Field claims and assumptions to preserve
- Proposed site is in New Cairo Industrial Zone, near VIBOTEL, in a large industrial area with many factories.
- Local claim: many buildings in the zone have fire-compliance issues and lack fire certificates, but lawyer/local contact says this will not prevent later IDA production-license application.
- Local lawyer/contact reportedly claims successful licensing can be contractually covered, with oral quote under 50,000 EGP for curtain-related production, subject to product/scale/pollution/noise complexity.
- Business-scope/address change is claimed to take within 3 weeks after factory lease.
- Factory renovation is expected to take about 2 months.
- Temporary production license after equipment arrival is claimed to take 2-5 days.
- Formal production license is claimed to take about 6 months.
- Temporary production license is claimed not to affect local Egypt sales, and possibly not export sales, but export impact is explicitly unverified.

- Other side is requesting more detailed facility data before payment, including door opening, floor height, and load-bearing data.

## Risk register from this meeting
- Risk: relying on oral assurances from lawyer/local friend about IDA/fire compliance.
  - Mitigation to request: written lease condition or agency contract stating required outcome, documents, refund/termination rights, and responsibility for failure.
- Risk: fire certificate absent or fire compliance materially weak.
  - Mitigation to request: list exact required fire/civil-defense documents; require engineer/fire consultant site walk; price upgrades before lease deposit.
- Risk: business-scope change and IDA path are assumed to be simpler than official dependencies suggest.
  - Mitigation to request: map the local lawyer's exact process against IDA/GOEIC/customs requirements.
- Risk: temporary production license claims may be misunderstood.
  - Mitigation to request: verify whether the cited document is operating license by notification, temporary/conditional industrial registration, or another administrative letter; confirm sales/export effect.
- Risk: equipment/layout mismatch.
  - Mitigation to request: freeze equipment list, dimensions, weights, door/route dimensions, floor load, electrical load, and installation layout before committing large deposit.
- Risk: address compliance and tax address change may take longer than business-scope change.
  - Mitigation to request: separate legal registered address, tax address, factory activity address, NAFEZA/import address, and IDA facility address in one table.
