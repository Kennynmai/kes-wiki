# Egypt Import Readiness and IDA Dependency Map

## Scope
Use this before shipping equipment or raw materials from China to a newly formed Egypt manufacturing company.

The question is not only whether the company exists and can use NAFEZA. The real dependency chain is whether the Egypt company can prove an industrial site, obtain or start IDA-facing approvals, and support customs release for production imports.

## Executive conclusion
If the project is a factory project, the first import shipment should not be treated as a pure customs/ACI task.

IDA-facing readiness matters before shipment because:
- IDA operating-license applications require a possession document for the facility.
- IDA approval of import invoices for production lines/equipment also requires a possession document and the invoice must show the industrial facility address, not merely the administrative company address.
- IDA approval of import invoices for startup raw materials / production supplies also requires a possession document.
- Customs registration for import for production requires official proof of productive/industrial/service activity or a GOEIC needs/production-supplies card.

Therefore, if only the tax card is ready and no factory/lease/possession document exists, the company may be incorporated but is not yet operationally ready to import equipment and inputs as a manufacturing project.

## Dependency chain

### 1. Company legal base
Required base:
- commercial register;
- tax card;
- VAT registration certificate or Form 8 / official VAT-status document;
- articles/company gazette or incorporation documents;
- authorized signer and POA/bank authorization if a representative acts.

Status interpretation:
- Tax card alone is insufficient.
- Commercial register and VAT status are needed for customs trader registration.
- Recent commercial register, tax card, company contract/gazette, and legal representative ID/passport are needed for e-Token/electronic seal.

### 2. Factory site / lease base
Before serious import execution, obtain a usable site basis:
- signed lease or ownership/allocation document;
- exact factory address, building/unit, floor, area, and activity;
- landlord ownership or right-to-lease evidence;
- fixed date / authentication / certification where required;
- landlord declarations on no building violations and no legal disputes where applicable;
- permission for manufacturing, machinery, fire/civil-defense work, electrical changes, storage, and equipment removal.

IDA's possession-document guide treats lease contracts as acceptable possession documents only with additional support, including date certification or authentication and evidence of the lessor's ownership/right relationship to the leased premises.

### 3. IDA activity and license path
Determine the IDA activity classification before importing:
- exact industrial activity and product scope;
- whether the operating license is by notification or prior approval;
- whether an accredited office route will be used.

Operating-license documents listed by IDA include:
- applicant identity;
- recent valid commercial register, printed within 6 months;
- POA or bank authorization if represented;
- possession document for the facility;
- previous operating license if any, or no-prohibition letter for certain facilities outside industrial zones;
- Federation of Egyptian Industries membership;
- technical and environmental file;
- fire-prevention report or civil-defense approval;
- tax card;
- consultant certificate that the building is fit for occupancy/use;
- company contact statement.

Timing:
- notification license: 1-5 business days;
- prior-approval license: 30 days after all necessary approvals are complete;
- accredited office route: same day, but requires the same underlying documents plus accredited office certificate.

### 4. IDA import-invoice approval for equipment
For importing production lines or parts of production lines for the first time or expansion, IDA has a service to approve import invoices and issue a letter to Customs.

Key documents:
- service form;
- possession document according to possession-document guides;
- no-prohibition letter from the governorate for first-time facilities outside industrial zones;
- original import invoice for machines/equipment, showing the name and industrial facility address receiving the machines, not the administrative headquarters address, or a bank-certified copy;
- packing list;
- bill of lading copy;
- valid recent commercial register;
- POA/bank authorization;
- applicant ID;
- for expansion: previous operating license / annual follow-up receipt / industrial register if any;
- for replacement/renewal: scrapping report from Industrial Control.

Important note:
- IDA requires the owner/representative to commit to apply to amend the operating license immediately after customs release of the machines/equipment.

Timing:
- 3 business days.

Implication:
- Equipment import can be linked to an IDA approval before full operation, but it still depends on possession document and industrial-site address.

### 5. IDA import-invoice approval for startup raw materials / production supplies
IDA also has a service to approve invoices for startup raw materials / production supplies and issue a letter to Customs.

Key documents:
- service form;
- possession document according to possession-document guides;
- no-prohibition letter from governorate for first-time facilities outside industrial zones;
- original raw-material invoice, showing the name and industrial facility address receiving the raw material, not the administrative headquarters address, bank-certified or bank-certified copy;
- packing list;
- bill of lading copy;
- valid recent commercial register;
- POA/bank authorization;
- applicant ID;
- for expansion: operating license / annual follow-up receipt / industrial register if any.

Timing:
- 3 business days.

Implication:
- Raw materials for startup should not be shipped before confirming whether this IDA invoice approval is needed for release or preferential treatment.

### 6. Industrial register after start
IDA's industrial register certificate is tied to the operating license and production start.

For first registration, IDA describes it as obtaining industrial registration within 30 days of starting production, with a 5-year registration period if documents are complete.

Key documents include:
- operating license and annual follow-up receipt if more than one year has passed;
- recent commercial register;
- tax card;
- Federation of Egyptian Industries membership with industrial facility address;
- POA/bank authorization;
- applicant ID.

Timing:
- 3 business days after technical opinion is received.

If lease is shorter than five years, IDA notes that a conditional industrial register may be appropriate for the same period stated in the lease.

## Go / no-go gates before shipment

## Timeline from factory selection / lease signing
This is the planning estimate from the day a factory is selected and the lease process starts.

The realistic answer is not one number. It depends on whether the lease is immediately IDA-usable, whether the activity is eligible for notification licensing, whether GOEIC/customs accept a temporary or startup-import route, and whether shipment is by sea or air.

### Fast but controlled case: about 7-10 weeks to receive cargo by sea
Assumptions:
- factory is already suitable for the intended activity;
- landlord can quickly provide ownership/right evidence and lease authentication support;
- activity falls under IDA notification route, not prior approval;
- customs broker confirms that equipment and startup raw materials can be released using IDA invoice approvals / temporary production route rather than waiting for full post-production industrial-register workflow;
- China exporter starts CargoX immediately and pays from the exporter company's own bank account;
- FCL sea freight is used.

Indicative sequence:
- Week 0-1: sign lease, authenticate/fix date, collect landlord ownership/right documents and declarations.
- Week 1-2: update/confirm commercial register, tax/VAT data, factory address, authorized signer, POA/bank authorization.
- Week 1-3: start NAFEZA/e-Token/customs trader registration and exporter CargoX verification in parallel.
- Week 2-4: prepare IDA import-invoice approval files for equipment and startup raw materials; obtain IDA approvals if required.
- Week 3-4: book vessel, confirm final invoice/packing list/HS codes, create ACID in NAFEZA, file CargoX ACI envelope.
- Week 4-5: load/ship from China.
- Week 8-9 or Week 9-10: arrival at Egypt port plus customs release and inland delivery if documents are clean.

### Normal first-time case: about 10-14 weeks by sea
Assumptions:
- factory lease needs legal/document cleanup;
- one or more of NAFEZA, e-Token, customs trader, IDA invoice approval, or GOEIC production-supplies route requires rework;
- first CargoX verification or bank remittance takes several business days;
- sea freight is FCL or simple LCL.

Indicative sequence:
- Weeks 0-2: lease completion, possession-document support, site/address consistency work.
- Weeks 1-4: company-file alignment, e-Token, NAFEZA, customs trader registration, GOEIC/production-input route confirmation.
- Weeks 3-6: IDA invoice approvals and any no-prohibition / outside-industrial-zone issue.
- Weeks 5-6: booking, ACID, CargoX ACI, final documents.
- Weeks 6-11 or 6-12: China-to-Egypt ocean transit.
- Weeks 11-14: discharge, inspection/customs release, inland delivery.

### Slow case: 14-24+ weeks
This is the risk case if:
- the factory is outside an industrial zone and needs a no-prohibition path;
- lease documents are not acceptable as IDA possession evidence;
- activity requires IDA prior approval or extra environmental/civil-defense work;
- commercial register, tax card, factory address, operating-license data, and GOEIC data do not match;
- GOEIC production-supplies registration cannot proceed without industrial register/operating license, and no temporary route is accepted by the broker/customs office;
- shipment is LCL, transshipment-heavy, or affected by port/route congestion.

In this case, do not ship raw materials until the customs-release basis is confirmed in writing.

### Air freight variant
Air freight can reduce physical transport to roughly one week, but it does not remove the Egypt-side regulatory path.

If all Egypt-side import approvals are already usable, first receipt by air may be possible in about 4-7 weeks from lease start. If the Egypt-side path is not ready, air freight only creates faster port/airport arrival and a higher risk of storage/demurrage while approvals are being fixed.

### Practical planning number
For budgeting the first sea shipment of machinery and raw materials from China to Egypt, use:
- aggressive target: 8 weeks from lease start to factory receipt;
- working plan: 12 weeks;
- conservative board-level date: 16 weeks;
- do not launch production planning on a sub-8-week promise unless the lease, IDA, customs, GOEIC, NAFEZA, CargoX, and HS-code paths have already been pre-cleared.

### Gate A: Before booking shipment
Must have:
- exporter CargoX onboarding started;
- Egypt importer company file confirmed;
- customs broker confirms import basis: production vs trade vs company use;
- HS code pre-check completed;
- IDA/GOEIC question answered for each category: equipment, raw material, accessories, spare parts;
- factory site selected or the team explicitly accepts importing under a non-production route.

### Gate B: Before shipping equipment
Must have:
- signed lease/possession document usable for IDA;
- invoice shows industrial facility address;
- IDA invoice-approval requirement checked or approval obtained;
- no-prohibition letter path checked if outside industrial zones;
- ACI/ACID process ready;
- customs trader registration ready or legally acceptable temporary path confirmed in writing.

### Gate C: Before shipping raw materials
Must have:
- production-input import route confirmed;
- IDA startup raw-material invoice approval requirement checked or approval obtained;
- GOEIC Production Supplies / needs-card issue resolved if required;
- storage and customs-release location confirmed;
- ACI/ACID process ready.

### Gate D: Before starting production
Must have:
- operating license or lawful temporary/notification path confirmed;
- fire/civil-defense and environmental documents resolved;
- building occupancy/suitability certificate;
- FEI membership if needed;
- industrial register timing planned after start.

## What to ask the Egypt team now
1. Do we have a recent commercial register, VAT certificate/Form 8, and tax card?
2. Has a factory site been selected? Is there a signed lease or only negotiation?
3. Will the lease be usable as `سند حيازة` under IDA's guide, including date certification/authentication and landlord ownership/right evidence?
4. Is the site inside an approved industrial zone, developer zone, or outside an industrial zone?
5. If outside an industrial zone, who will obtain the no-prohibition letter?
6. What exact IDA industrial activity code will be used?
7. Is the operating license path notification or prior approval?
8. Does equipment import require IDA invoice approval before customs release?
9. Does startup raw-material import require IDA invoice approval before customs release?
10. Are invoices being issued to the industrial facility address rather than the administrative registered address?
11. Does the customs broker require GOEIC Production Supplies Registry / needs card?
12. Can any temporary customs registration be used if full industrial documentation is not ready, and who approves it?
