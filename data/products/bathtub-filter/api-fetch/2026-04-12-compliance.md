# API Output — Bathtub Filter Compliance Notes

## Current standards/compliance observations

- NSF/ANSI 177 is the most visible shower-filtration benchmark in surfaced materials.
- NSF consumer guidance states shower filters certified to NSF/ANSI 177 are certified to reduce only free available chlorine.
- This creates a key claim-discipline issue for bathtub filters and bath-ball products: brands may imply broader reduction or skin-outcome effects than the standard itself supports.
- The category appears full of mixed wording such as 'certified to', 'tested to', 'independently tested', and plain performance claims. These need to be separated carefully in future audits.
