# Bathtub Filter Installation Risk Matrix

## Version links
- Newer working page: [[bathtub-filter-installation-risk-matrix-v2]]

## Matrix
| Risk area | Why it matters | Current signal | Risk level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Faucet geometry mismatch | tub spouts vary; shape/clearance matter | community + explainer content repeatedly imply compatibility dependence | high |
| Weak attachment stability | hanging/ball systems can slip or wobble | Amazon question snippets and review-style comments surfaced slipping concerns | high |
| Leak / seam failure | destroys trust immediately | adjacent shower-filter review snippets repeatedly mention seam/housing leaks | high |
| Fill-speed penalty | users expect a tub to fill in reasonable time | review/testing content repeatedly shows slower flow often required for better chlorine reduction | high |
| Drying / storage burden | soft/hanging systems need care between uses | especially relevant for Santevia-style format | medium |
| “Universal fit” overclaim | fragile promise across varied bathroom setups | appears common in marketing, but not strongly proven | high |

## Working conclusion
Installation and compatibility are not minor details in this category; they may be one of the main reasons a technically plausible product still fails commercially.
